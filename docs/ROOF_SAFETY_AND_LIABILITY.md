# Roof safety + condition liability

> **Not legal advice.** This is an internal working note plus *draft* wording. The
> WHS position and the contract/notice clauses below must be reviewed and signed off
> by a qualified WHS adviser and a lawyer before use.

## Two different liabilities — do not conflate them
A single "non-liability clause" does NOT cover both of these. They are separate risks
with separate treatments.

### 1. Worker safety — a sales tech climbing a roof (the "burn in court" risk)
This is a **Work Health and Safety** duty, and it **cannot be contracted out**.
- Under the model WHS Act, a PCBU must manage the risk of a fall (Reg 78), and working
  at height is a recognised high-risk activity. If an untrained person is enabled or
  directed to work at height and is injured, the PCBU is exposed to WHS prosecution
  (up to industrial/workplace-manslaughter territory) **and** common-law negligence.
- **WHS Act s272 prohibits contracting out**: a duty cannot be transferred — not to the
  worker, not to the customer, not to a subcontractor. A waiver, a sales-contract clause,
  or a signed acknowledgment does **nothing** to remove this liability.
- **The only real control is elimination**: sales techs never access the roof. Full stop.

**Policy (enforced in the app):**
- The sales tech captures the roof from **ground level, a drone, or Pylon/satellite
  imagery — never by getting on the roof** (no ladders onto the roof, no walking it).
- The AI photo checker is instructed to **never advise climbing**; a "close-up" always
  means a ground zoom or drone frame.
- Roof access for any purpose is a **licensed installer** activity with the appropriate
  height-safety training, equipment and SWMS — not a sales function.

### 2. Roof condition — the customer's asset
This one a clause **can** properly address, backed by a real process:
- ~9/10 the **ground shot + Pylon satellite** establishes the roof material (tin vs tile),
  which is all the quote needs. The sales visit does **not** certify roof condition.
- **Roof condition is an installation-stage gate**: the licensed installer inspects and
  verifies the roof's condition/suitability on the day, as part of the mandatory install
  evidence (photo set + completion report). If the roof is unsafe/unsuitable, a skilled
  person catches it then — before any load or penetration.

## Draft wording (for legal review) — pick ONE primary route, or both
The business preference is to make clear me-solar/Solarsearch does not warrant roof
condition from the sales assessment. Two mechanisms:

### Option A — customer sales-contract clause
> **Roof condition.** The quotation and design are based on a non-invasive assessment
> (ground-level photographs and satellite imagery) and do not include an inspection or
> certification of the condition, structural adequacy or watertightness of the roof.
> [me-solar/Solarsearch] does not warrant the condition of the existing roof. The
> roof's condition and suitability to receive the installation are assessed by the
> licensed installer at the time of installation; if the roof is found to be unsuitable,
> the installer will advise the customer and works may be paused or varied. The customer
> is responsible for the pre-existing condition of their roof and any rectification of
> pre-existing defects.

### Option B — installer notification / engagement terms
> **Roof condition is the installer's on-site verification.** The site assessment
> supplied to you (photos, satellite imagery, roof-material read) is provided as a design
> aid and does **not** certify roof condition, structure or watertightness. As the
> licensed installer you are responsible for inspecting and verifying the roof's condition
> and suitability on site, using your own height-safety controls, before commencing.
> Record the roof condition in the install evidence (photo set + completion report).
> [me-solar/Solarsearch] does not warrant roof condition and relies on your on-site
> verification.

**Note:** Option B (installer verification + evidence) is the stronger, process-backed
position because it puts the condition call with the licensed person who is actually on
the roof. Option A protects against the *customer's* pre-existing-defect claims. They are
complementary — a lawyer may well advise doing both. Neither touches risk #1 above.

## Process hooks
- **Capture (sales):** ground/drone/satellite only; AI reads roof *material* for the
  quote; condition is a best-effort heads-up, never a blocker; no roof access.
- **Install (gate):** licensed installer verifies roof condition/suitability and records
  it in the mandatory install photo set + completion report before load/penetration.
