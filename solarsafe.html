What we check

Every critical component, photographed and assessed.

Non-intrusive by design — we photograph accessible components only. Anything that needs enclosures opened or testing goes to a licensed electrician, and we tell you exactly why.

☀️
Rooftop array

Panel condition, cracking, discolouration, mounting, flashing and penetrations.

🔌
DC isolators

The most common failure point in Australian solar — housing, seals, labelling.

📟
Inverter

Operation, error history on display, age, and battery compatibility.

⚡
Switchboard

Exterior assessment: main switch rating, spare capacity for a battery circuit, labelling.

〰️
Cabling & conduit

UV damage, sagging, mechanical protection along visible runs.

🏷️
Signage & labelling

Required shutdown procedure and warning labels, legible and in place.

🔋
Battery readiness

Location options, clearances, inverter pathway (hybrid vs AC-coupled).

🔎
Installed batteries

Location and clearances against AS/NZS 5139, impact protection, signage, shutdown procedure, visible condition.

📄
Paperwork check

What certificates and documentation you should hold — and what's missing.

Adding a battery? Check the house can take one first.

The federal battery discount steps down every six months — but a battery bought before checking your inverter, switchboard and wall space is how people end up with surprise costs on install day. Thousands of batteries are going in every week to catch the rebate. Speed is fine — shortcuts aren't. An independent check while your workmanship warranty is fresh costs $249; finding a clearance problem in year five costs a lot more.

If we find something

We gather the evidence. You hold the power.

Our only contact with your installer is a single email notice of the findings, sent on your written authority. Everything else is evidence gathering and paperwork — a complete, date-stamped file that puts you in the strongest position, with a clear path shown at every step. You decide; you act; we make sure your file is unarguable.

1
One email notice of rectification

Findings with photo evidence and standard references, emailed to your installer on your authority with a 14-day response window. That is the only contact we make — no calls, no chasing. Most issues end here; reputable installers fix their work.

2
Escalation — your call, your name, our paperwork

If they refuse or don't respond, we prepare the complete complaint pack for you to lodge with the Clean Energy Regulator (for accredited installs) and NSW Fair Trading — including your claim for reimbursement of the inspection cost where the original installer is at fault. We show the way and hand you the file; lodging is yours. The evidence does the talking.

3
The evidence file

Every photo, finding, letter and response, date-stamped from day one. If it goes to a tribunal, your file is already built.

Straight talk: Solarsafe is not a regulator and never pretends to be — our only installer contact is the single email notice of rectification, sent on your authority; escalation documents are prepared for you to lodge yourself. Solarsafe and Solarsearch (our quote-comparison service) are related businesses, and we disclose that on every report. Findings are what the photos show — an accredited assessor signs every one, and no finding is ever influenced by whether you buy anything else from us. You pay the same flat fee whatever we find: our fee never depends on findings, and where reimbursement of your inspection cost is pursued from an at-fault installer, that is your claim, made in your name — not our revenue.
